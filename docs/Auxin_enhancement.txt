🌱 AUXIN SYSTEM — CONCEPTUAL ROADMAP

Think of this as the real-world system you’re trying to approximate, not an implementation.

🧠 1. What auxin is in the plant

At a high level:

Auxin is a directional signaling field that encodes “where growth should be prioritized” and “which structures dominate.”

It is not:

energy
a static concentration field
a global broadcast signal

It is:

produced locally
transported directionally
self-reinforcing along successful paths
🌿 2. Where auxin comes from (production system)

Auxin production is not centralized.

Primary sources:
- Apical meristems (shoot tips) → strong, continuous
- Young, expanding leaves → distributed, moderate
Weak/negligible sources:
- Mature leaves
- Old tissue

Key consequence:
A large branch produces more auxin not because it’s long,
but because it has more active growing tissue near its tip.

So dominance scales with:

number of active sites
not distance from the root

🚚 3. How auxin moves (transport system)

Auxin transport is:

Directional (polar)
Strong bias downward (shoot → root)
Weak upward/backflow
Active (not diffusion)
Controlled by transport proteins (PINs)
Cells choose where auxin exits
Key consequence

Auxin forms a persistent gradient, not an equilibrium field

High near tips
Lower toward roots
Never equalizes

🔁 4. Canalization (the core dynamic)

This is the most important mechanism.

Auxin flow reinforces its own transport pathways

Biologically:

Cells experiencing auxin flux increase transport capacity in that direction

System-level effect:

Frequently used paths become easier to flow through
Rarely used paths weaken
Key consequence

The plant builds a transport network shaped by history of flow

This is how you get:

trunks
dominant branches
stable architecture
🌳 5. Two layers of transport (critical distinction)
Fast layer — signaling (auxin conductance)
Changes quickly
Reversible
Determines routing of auxin
Slow layer — structure (vascular tissue)
Builds over time
Persistent
Determines capacity for:
water
sugar
nutrients
Key consequence

Auxin doesn’t just move through the plant—it builds the highways that everything else uses later.

⚖️ 6. Competition between branches

At any junction:

Branches compete based on how effectively they export auxin into the shared network

Not:

who is closest
who has highest local concentration

But:

who has the strongest flow + transport capacity
Positive feedback loop
Slight advantage in production or transport
→ more auxin flow
→ stronger transport path
→ even more flow
Key consequence

Small differences become structural dominance

🌞 7. Interaction with light and sugar (your new systems)

Auxin does not directly sense light.

Instead:

Light → Sugar → Growth → Auxin → Transport → Resource allocation
What actually happens
Better-lit branch:
produces more sugar
grows more
creates more young tissue
produces more auxin
strengthens its transport path
Key consequence

Auxin translates resource success into structural dominance

🌿 8. Apical dominance (branch suppression)

Auxin from a dominant tip:

suppresses nearby axillary buds
prevents excessive branching near the top

Mechanism:

high auxin → low relative cytokinin effect → buds stay dormant
Key consequence

The plant maintains a hierarchical structure, not a bush of equal branches

🌱 9. Node creation (plastochron + auxin spacing)

Node formation is controlled by:

Timing (plastochron)
internal rhythm at meristem
modulated by sugar and conditions
Local auxin depletion
existing primordia create local sinks
prevents new nodes forming too close
Key consequence

The plant creates regular opportunities for growth, but spacing emerges from local interactions

⚠️ 10. What auxin does not do (important constraints)
It does NOT:
act like a long-range attractor
equalize via diffusion
get significantly drained by primordia sinks
maintain constant concentration over distance
It DOES:
operate via flow, not static levels
encode relative dominance
depend on network structure
🧠 11. The real system in one sentence

Auxin is a self-organizing, flow-based signaling system that converts local growth success into global structural dominance via transport reinforcement.

🌳 12. What realism looks like when you get this right

Your system should naturally produce:

One branch becoming dominant without explicit rules
Crown shifting toward better light
Stable trunks that don’t collapse with distance
Gradual takeover when conditions change
Weak branches stalling or dying (with sugar coupling)
🔥 Final insight (most important takeaway)

Auxin is not about “how much is here”
It’s about how effectively a region participates in the plant-wide flow network

That’s the piece that usually gets missed.
